Anyone can claim a record.
Few can prove one.

A marketplace for football forecasts where track records are graded by cryptographic proofs on Solana — never by the platform, never by the seller.

01 · Problem

Paid picks run on unverifiable claims

Deleted losses

Losing calls vanish from channels minutes after full time. Buyers only ever see the wins.

Backdated wins

Screenshots are produced after results are known. No timestamp anyone can check.

Captured referees

Rating platforms grade their own sellers. The referee works for one of the teams.

Result: honest analysts are priced like frauds, and buyers pay for confidence instead of skill.

02 · Solution

Seal it. Prove it. Bond it.

Pick hashed + anchored on Solana before kickoff→ Revealed + checked against the sealed hash→ Graded by TxLINE Merkle proofs→ Bond refunds subscribers if the floor breaks
  • Every analyst stakes a USDC bond behind a published accuracy floor.
  • Settlement pulls stat-validation proofs from TxLINE; the eventStatRoot travels with every grade.
  • No user ever wagers on a match — the product is verified expertise, not exposure.

03 · Why now, why Solana

The referee has to be neutral

  • A Web2 platform grading its own tipsters is precisely the fraud being solved — the trust model requires settlement no party controls.
  • TxLINE anchors daily Merkle roots of World Cup data on Solana and exposes validate_stat for trustless outcome checks via CPI.
  • Commit-reveal, bond vaults, and automatic refunds are cheap, fast, and final on Solana.
